How our cabinets are built

Anyone can show you a pretty door. We want you to see what's behind it — the materials, joinery and hardware that make a Saguaro cabinet last for decades.

Quality you can feel

Built to last — not built to a price

A cabinet gets opened, slammed, loaded and leaned on thousands of times a year. The difference between one that still feels solid in twenty years and one that sags in two comes down to what you can't see: the box, the joinery, and the hardware.

Here's exactly what goes into the cabinets we build and install, point by point.

Point by point

What goes into every cabinet

The cabinet box

Every box is built from furniture-grade plywood and finished with a UV clear coat that seals the wood and stands up to humidity and daily use. Plywood resists the sagging and swelling that particleboard is prone to, so shelves stay flat and joints stay tight.

Solid wood face frames

Thick solid wood face frames with 1½-inch stiles and rails give the cabinet its strength and a furniture-quality front. Plywood side panels dado directly into the face frame, with a ¼-inch side reveal before the finished skin is applied — a joinery method that keeps the whole cabinet square and rigid.

Solid wood corner brackets

Solid wood corner brackets are added inside the box to keep each cabinet perfectly square and add extra stability — important for carrying the weight of stone countertops and for a clean, gap-free install.

Dovetail drawer boxes

Drawer boxes are solid wood with dovetail joinery on all four sides — the strongest way to join a drawer — plus a plywood bottom and a UV clear coat. Dovetails won't pull apart like stapled or glued-only boxes do over years of use.

Steel soft-close glides

Drawers ride on all-steel, soft-close glides. They carry more weight, pull smoother, and close themselves the last inch with a quiet, controlled motion — no slamming.

6-way soft-close hinges

Doors hang on 6-way adjustable, soft-close hinges. The six-way adjustment lets us dial in perfect alignment and even reveals; the soft-close keeps doors from banging and keeps them looking right for years.

Adjustable plywood shelves

Shelves are plywood finished with a birch wood veneer top and bottom, and edge-banded on the front leading edge for a clean, durable look. They're fully adjustable, so you can set the spacing to fit what you actually store.

Finish & details

A UV clear coat protects the interior, and toe kick boards are a sturdy 4½-inch high, 2½-inch deep and ½-inch thick. Small details — but they're the ones that separate a custom cabinet from a flat-pack box.

The short version

Our build standards

  • Furniture-grade plywood boxes with UV clear coat
  • Solid wood face frames, 1½" stiles & rails
  • Solid wood dovetail drawer boxes
  • All-steel soft-close drawer glides
  • 6-way adjustable soft-close hinges
  • Edge-banded, adjustable plywood shelves
  • Solid wood corner brackets for a square, stable box
